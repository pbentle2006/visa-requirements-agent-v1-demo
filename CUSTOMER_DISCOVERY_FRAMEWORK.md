# Customer Discovery Interview Framework

## üéØ Objective

Validate problem-solution fit, gather feature requirements, and identify pilot opportunities for the Visa Requirements Agent system.

## üìã Interview Structure (45-60 minutes)

### Phase 1: Problem Discovery (15 minutes)

#### Current State Analysis
**"Tell me about your current process for handling immigration policy changes."**

**Follow-up Questions:**
- How often do you need to analyze new or updated immigration policies?
- What's your typical timeline from policy release to implementation?
- Who is involved in this process? (roles, departments, external consultants)
- What tools or systems do you currently use?

**Pain Point Identification:**
- What's the most challenging part of this process?
- Where do you see the most errors or delays occurring?
- How do policy changes impact your operations/clients?
- What would happen if you could reduce this timeline by 90%?

**Cost & Resource Impact:**
- How much time does your team spend on policy analysis per month?
- What's the typical cost (internal + external) for implementing a new policy?
- How many policies do you handle per year?
- What's the business impact of delays in policy implementation?

### Phase 2: Solution Validation (20 minutes)

#### Demo & Feedback
**"Let me show you how our AI system handles this process."**

**Demo Script:**
1. Upload sample policy document
2. Show real-time agent workflow
3. Display generated requirements and questions
4. Highlight validation scores and traceability

**Validation Questions:**
- Does this output match what you'd expect from your current process?
- What's missing that you'd need to see?
- How does the quality compare to your current results?
- Would this level of automation be valuable to your organization?

**Feature Prioritization:**
- Which part of this workflow would be most valuable to automate first?
- What additional features would make this a must-have solution?
- How important is integration with your existing systems?
- What compliance or security requirements would we need to meet?

### Phase 3: Commercial Validation (15 minutes)

#### Buying Process & Decision Making
**"If this solution could deliver the results we've shown, how would your organization evaluate it?"**

**Decision Process:**
- Who would be involved in evaluating this type of solution?
- What's your typical procurement process for new technology?
- What budget range would be appropriate for this type of efficiency gain?
- How do you typically measure ROI for process automation tools?

**Implementation Readiness:**
- How quickly could you move forward with a pilot program?
- What would success look like in a 30-day pilot?
- What internal resources would you need to allocate?
- Are there any regulatory or compliance hurdles we should know about?

### Phase 4: Next Steps (5 minutes)

#### Pilot Program Discussion
**"Based on what you've seen, would you be interested in a pilot program?"**

**Pilot Scoping:**
- What specific use case would be best for a pilot?
- What timeline would work for your organization?
- Who would be the internal champion for this project?
- What would constitute a successful pilot outcome?

## üìä Interview Tracking & Analysis

### Interview Scorecard

**Problem Validation (1-5 scale):**
- [ ] Problem severity (how painful is current process?)
- [ ] Problem frequency (how often does this occur?)
- [ ] Current solution inadequacy (how well do existing tools work?)
- [ ] Willingness to change (openness to new solutions?)

**Solution Validation (1-5 scale):**
- [ ] Feature relevance (does our solution address their needs?)
- [ ] Quality assessment (does output meet their standards?)
- [ ] Competitive advantage (is this better than alternatives?)
- [ ] Implementation feasibility (can they adopt this solution?)

**Commercial Validation (1-5 scale):**
- [ ] Budget availability (do they have funds for this?)
- [ ] Decision authority (can this person influence purchase?)
- [ ] Timeline urgency (when do they need a solution?)
- [ ] Pilot readiness (would they start a pilot program?)

### Key Metrics to Track

**Quantitative Data:**
- Current process timeline (weeks/months)
- Team size involved in policy analysis
- Annual policy volume handled
- Current cost per policy implementation
- Error rates and rework frequency

**Qualitative Insights:**
- Primary pain points and frustrations
- Must-have vs. nice-to-have features
- Integration requirements and constraints
- Competitive landscape and alternatives
- Decision-making process and timeline
